Chicken Shawarma

Description

Chicken shawarma is a popular Middle Eastern dish made by marinating thin slices of chicken in a flavorful blend of spices such as cumin, paprika, turmeric, garlic, and lemon juice. The marinated meat is traditionally stacked on a vertical rotisserie and slowly cooked as it rotates, allowing the outer layers to become crispy while remaining tender and juicy inside. Once cooked, the meat is shaved off in thin slices and often served wrapped in pita bread or flatbreads with accompaniments like tahini sauce, garlic sauce, fresh vegetables, and pickles.

This dish is loved for its rich, smoky flavor and versatility, making it a common street food and restaurant favorite across many countries. Shawarma can be customized with different toppings and sauces to suit various tastes, and it is often enjoyed as a quick meal or part of a larger spread in Middle Eastern cuisine. Its balance of spices and textures offers a satisfying combination of savory, tangy, and fresh flavors.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs (or breasts), thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground paprika
  • 1 tablespoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt (optional, for tenderness)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine olive oil, garlic, cumin, paprika, turmeric, coriander, cinnamon, black pepper, salt, cayenne, lemon juice, and yogurt if using. Mix well to make the marinade.
  2. Add the sliced chicken to the marinade, making sure all pieces are well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably 4 hours or overnight for best flavor.
  3. Preheat a grill, grill pan, or skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Cook the chicken slices in batches for about 5-7 minutes per side until fully cooked and slightly charred at the edges.
  5. Remove from heat and let rest for a few minutes.
  6. Serve the chicken shawarma sliced thinly, wrapped in pita bread or flatbread with toppings such as tahini sauce, garlic sauce, chopped tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, and pickles.
