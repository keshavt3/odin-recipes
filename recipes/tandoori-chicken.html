Tandoori Chicken

Description

Tandoori chicken is a flavorful dish originating from the Indian subcontinent, characterized by its vibrant red hue and smoky aroma. The chicken is marinated in a mixture of yogurt and spices, including garam masala, cumin, cayenne pepper, and turmeric. Traditionally, it is cooked in a tandoor, a cylindrical clay oven fueled by charcoal or wood, which imparts a distinctive smoky flavor and achieves a high cooking temperature that sears the chicken, locking in moisture.

This dish is often served as an appetizer or main course, accompanied by naan, rice, or a cooling yogurt-based raita. While the tandoor method is traditional, modern adaptations include grilling or baking the chicken to replicate the high-heat cooking process. Tandoori chicken's popularity has spread globally, with variations like chicken tikka and chicken tikka masala emerging from this culinary tradition.

Steps

  1. Prepare the marinade. Add the yogurt to a mixing bowl. Add the spices, freshly minced ginger and garlic, and olive oil.
  2. Marinate. Pat dry the chicken pieces and put them in the marinade. Mix well to get the chicken completely coated. Let the chicken sit for at least 10 minutes.
  3. Assemble. Remove the chicken from the yogurt mixture and shake off the excess marinade. Place the chicken on the wire rack.
  4. Bake for 25 to 30 minutes or until the chicken is nicely charred and cooked through.
